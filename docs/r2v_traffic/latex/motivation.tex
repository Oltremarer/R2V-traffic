\paragraph{Motivation.}
Traffic signal control agents learn from replayed transitions, but uniform replay and generic prioritization do not distinguish traffic rarity from traffic usefulness. Rare queue or pressure patterns can expose important coordination failures, yet they can also be unsafe, off-support, or simply unhelpful. We therefore treat rarity as a candidate discovery signal rather than a value label. R2V-Traffic decomposes replay improvement into proposal and admission: a generative or scoring backend proposes candidate replay mass, while independent support, OOD/value, and dynamics gates decide whether the candidate should influence training.
